Your First 48 Hours in Nairobi — A Practical Guide by KIMANZI

By KIMANZI · Coordination layer for UrbanChill in Nairobi

Arriving in Nairobi for the first time can feel exciting, fast-moving, and unfamiliar. The city is energetic, layered, and full of opportunity — but the first 48 hours matter. They set the tone for how quickly you settle, how confident you feel, and how smoothly your work begins.

At KIMANZI, we coordinate the arrival support for UrbanChill professionals. We work with Nairobi locals who know the city well and guide newcomers through their first days. This article outlines what those first 48 hours typically look like, and how hosts help newcomers land comfortably.

Day 1 — Arrival and Orientation

Airport arrival

Most newcomers arrive at Jomo Kenyatta International Airport (JKIA). A host meets them at the agreed location, helps them navigate the arrival process, and ensures they reach their accommodation safely. No uncertainty about taxis, routes, or where to go first.

Neighbourhood basics

Once settled, hosts walk newcomers through the essentials:

  • Nearby shops and supermarkets
  • Transport options (Uber, boda-boda, trusted drivers)
  • Safety considerations per neighbourhood
  • Local norms and practical tips for moving around the city
  • What to expect in the first morning

This early orientation reduces uncertainty and helps newcomers feel grounded before their first working day.

Day 2 — Practical Setup

SIM and M-Pesa

A working SIM and M-Pesa account are non-negotiable for functioning in Nairobi. Hosts guide newcomers through the registration process — what to bring, where to go, and how to complete the first transaction. What could take a newcomer half a day alone takes an hour with a host who knows the process.

Local registrations

Some newcomers need help with KRA registration, mobile setup, or other practical steps. Hosts guide them through the process, explain what is needed, and help them complete the steps efficiently.

Daily logistics

This can include:

  • Finding a reliable taxi or boda service
  • Understanding payment methods (cash, M-Pesa, card)
  • Navigating common routes without losing an hour to traffic
  • Identifying trusted local services — pharmacy, grocery, bank

These small but important details make a significant difference in confidence and independence from day two onwards.

Why Local Hosts Matter

Nairobi is a city best understood through people who live it every day. A host does not provide a tourist experience. They provide clarity, calm, and practical guidance — the kind that only comes from knowing which route to take at which hour, which supermarket opens early, and which registration desk to avoid on a Monday morning.

UrbanChill professionals consistently say that host support makes their first days smoother, safer, and more productive. The difference between arriving with a host and arriving without one is often measured in days — days that would otherwise be spent figuring out what a local already knows.
