🚗 Getting around
🚗Uber & Bolt
Always use Uber or Bolt — safer and price-fixed. Never take unmarked taxis. Share your ride status with the client if travelling at night.
🏍️Boda Boda (motorcycle taxi)
Avoid with clients Bodas are fast but risky. Only use for quick personal errands, not client transport.
🗺️Navigation
Use Google Maps for routes. Traffic in Nairobi is heavy 7–9am and 5–8pm — plan ahead. Westlands, Karen and Kilimani are the main client areas.
💰 Money & payments
📱M-PESA
M-PESA is king. Most shops, restaurants and services accept it. Always send your M-PESA confirmation to KIMANZI for reimbursement.
🏧ATMs & cash
Use ATMs inside malls (Westgate, Village Market, The Hub Karen) — safer than street ATMs. Equity Bank and KCB have reliable machines.
💱Exchange rate
EUR 1 ≈ KES 145–155 (check live rate in your portal). Forex bureaus give better rates than banks — try JM Forex in Westlands.
📶 SIM & data
📱Getting a SIM
Best operators: Safaricom Airtel

Safaricom has the best coverage. Clients need their passport for registration. SIM costs ~KES 50, buy at any Safaricom shop or kiosk.

Airport: Safaricom desk is in the arrivals hall — get it on day 1.
🔒 Safety
✅Safe areas
Westlands Kilimani Karen Lavington Runda

These are the main expat areas — well-lit, good security, close to restaurants and shops.
⚠️General rules
• Don't walk with expensive items visible (phone, camera, laptop bag)
• Avoid walking alone after dark — use Uber
• Lock car doors immediately when entering
• Don't resist if robbed — safety first
• Share your location with someone when meeting clients for the first time
🍽️ Food & dining
🥗Recommended spots
Casual: Artcaffe, Java House, Kaldis Coffee
Local food: Mama Oliech, Kikopey Nyama Choma
Supermarkets: Carrefour, Naivas, QuickMart

Vegan/vegetarian Choices Restaurant (Westlands), Harvest (Karen)
💧Water
Always drink bottled water. Tap water is not safe for newcomers. Buy 5L bottles at any supermarket — much cheaper than small bottles.
