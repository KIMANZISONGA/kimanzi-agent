Frequently asked questions

Practical answers for independent professionals considering hosting with KIMANZI.

What does a KIMANZI host actually do?

A host supports UrbanChill professionals during their first days in Nairobi. You carry out clear, time-bound assignments that help newcomers settle quickly and safely. This can include arrival support, practical guidance, neighbourhood orientation, or helping with local registrations.

Is this a job or employment?

No. You work independently. You choose which assignments you accept. There is no employment contract, no minimum hours, and no ongoing obligations.

How long does an assignment take?

Most assignments take between 1 and 3 hours. Some arrival-day assignments may take longer depending on the newcomer's needs. The scope is always stated clearly before you accept.

Do I need a car?

Not always. Some assignments involve transport, but many do not. If an assignment requires a vehicle, this will be stated clearly upfront.

How does payment work?

Each assignment has a fixed fee, confirmed before you accept. After completing the assignment, log your receipts in your host portal — a photo per expense, submitted in seconds from your phone. Payment runs within 2 working days via M-Pesa (or another method on request). Concierge commissions run monthly.

Do I need to front any costs?

No. Airport transfer is arranged and prepaid by KIMANZI. Fuel and lunch you advance on the day and log in your host portal — reimbursed in full.

Do I need special training or certificates?

No. Your value is your local knowledge of Nairobi, your reliability, and your ability to communicate clearly. No diplomas required.

Who are the newcomers I will meet?

UrbanChill professionals arriving in Nairobi for work — consultants, NGO staff, remote workers, entrepreneurs. They are independent, respectful, and appreciative of local guidance. They are not on holiday.

How do I know what to do during an assignment?

Every assignment comes with a full briefing: guest profile, flight info, accommodation address, and practical notes. You will know exactly what is expected, how long it takes, and what the newcomer needs. Your route, your timing, your judgement.

Can I decline assignments?

Yes, every time, without explanation. Declining one assignment has no effect on whether we offer you the next.

Do I have to be available on a schedule?

No. The availability question on the form helps us know roughly when not to call. You're free to be unavailable for weeks at a time, or to take a long break and pick up later.

What if a guest asks something outside the assignment?

Do it only if you're comfortable, and let KIMANZI know. A quick message is enough. This keeps the picture complete so we can invoice correctly and make sure you're covered.

How often will I get assignments?

Honest answer: we don't promise a volume. Some months are quiet, some are busy. KIMANZI is meant to be an additional channel, not a primary income source.

How do I sign up?

Submit your interest through the form on the homepage. We respond within one working day. If your profile fits, we set up a short call.
