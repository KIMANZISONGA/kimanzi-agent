What KIMANZI coordinates

Practical, time-boxed, in-person work

KIMANZI assignments are time-boxed and concrete. A half-day, two half-days across an Arrival, or a month of light remote support. The work is practical: meet someone at JKIA, hand them their keys, take them to the supermarket, walk them through Westlands, drive them to KRA. No mystery, no atmosphere talk. KIMANZI assignments support newcomers during their Nairobi onboarding — practical relocation help that makes their first days smooth and safe.

Arrival days most common

Arrival runs across two half-days. Evening or night: pickup at JKIA, transfer to accommodation, short briefing on essentials. Next morning: SIM card, M-Pesa setup, supermarket run, coworking tour, neighborhood orientation. The guest lands and rests first — the setup follows when they're ready.

Extra days

A half-day assignment, on weekdays. Booked alongside an Arrival or standalone when the guest needs focused time on a specific task: housing visits, paperwork at KRA or immigration, a school visit, a bank appointment. The host sets the order and pace.

Concierge follow-up

Concierge is your recurring income. Once a guest subscribes, the work is light and mostly remote — a WhatsApp question, a vendor tip, the occasional task — and your commission runs every month for as long as they stay. More on how this builds just below.

Special requests

Occasionally a guest asks for something custom. A multi-day trip out of Nairobi, an introduction to a specific community, deeper logistical support. We scope these per request and discuss with the host before confirming.

Recurring income

Concierge — the income that builds on itself

Every guest you help settle can become a subscriber. Once they do, the work turns light — a WhatsApp question, a vendor tip, the occasional task — and your commission runs every month for as long as they stay. Mostly remote. A response within the working day, not on-call.

The point isn't one guest. It's what accumulates. Each subscriber you keep is one you don't have to earn again — a book of recurring guests you build over time, paying out month after month while you take on new arrivals alongside it. Three active Concierge guests covers your rent. The hosts who treat this seriously don't stop there.

This is the part of the model that rewards staying good at it. Guests renew with people they trust, and trust is exactly what you're bringing.

Why it matters

What you actually change

A guest's first morning decides a lot. Handled badly, it sets the tone for a week spent tired and behind. Handled well, it becomes part of arriving — coffee that's already there, a route they trust, a city that suddenly feels manageable.

That shift is your work. Not the logistics on paper, but what the logistics do: someone starts their week feeling competent instead of lost. Guests rarely forget the person who gave them that. You won't be promoted publicly — that's not how we work — but the guests know, and so do we.

A small group of hosts is already doing this across Nairobi. We stay selective on purpose. If you're the kind of person whose steadiest hour is someone else's most disorienting one, you belong in that group.

Behind KIMANZI

Why I started this

Travelling showed me that the first hours in a new place can feel heavy: confusion, hunger, noise, fatigue. Too much at once.

You walk into your apartment after a long flight. There's coffee, there's water. No bread, no yoghurt, nothing for a simple breakfast. So instead of resting, you head out again, past unfamiliar streets into a supermarket where the brands are different and the queue takes longer than you expected. Or you've landed at eleven at night, and the last thing you want to work out is what's still open, which route is safe at this hour, and what tomorrow morning will ask of you before it's even started.

You wanted to land. You're still landing.

These are small things. Not catastrophes. But the small things are what decide whether a first week starts with someone feeling steady, or already tired and behind.

For a long time I could see the problem clearly but not the answer. When it came, it wasn't a system or an app. It was a person. Someone already in Nairobi who knows which shop opens early, which neighbourhood grocery stocks the brand a newcomer would want, which route runs safe after dark. Someone whose calm holds at exactly the hour a stranger is most lost. I couldn't be that person in every neighbourhood, on every arrival. But you can.

That's what a host is. UrbanChill takes those small things off a guest's plate; KIMANZI is how it actually happens on the ground, through you. When a guest's first morning turns out well, that isn't luck and it isn't logistics. That's you. The guest may never say it out loud, but I always know where it came from.

So I build this slowly, and I stay selective. Not to keep people out, but because to me a host isn't help I hire. You're the operation. I'd rather have one careful conversation than a rushed signup, and I'd rather decline a partnership than dilute what you'd be part of. If you're considering applying as a host, partnering with us in Kenya, or setting up an arrangement from the Netherlands, send a message and we'll talk.

— Stephen L.Founder, UrbanChill & KIMANZI

FAQ

Frequent questions from hosts

For hosts

Is this employment?

No. You operate as an independent professional. You invoice KIMANZI per assignment and you decide what other work you take on. There is no exclusivity, no minimum availability and no schedule commitment.

What if a guest contacts me directly for new work?

New assignments from UrbanChill guests run through KIMANZI. Not because of a contract clause — but because that's how the model works. You bring the expertise, we bring the guests. Both sides need the other to make this worthwhile. If a guest reaches out to you directly for something new, point them back to UrbanChill and let us know. We handle it from there.

How is payment handled?

Right after each completed assignment, open your host portal and log your receipts there — a photo per expense, submitted in seconds from your phone. Your total updates immediately, so you always know exactly what's pending. No invoice to draft, no email to send. Once we confirm completion, payout goes out within 2 working days.

The fast turnaround is structural, not a service promise: assignments only enter the pipeline after the guest has paid KIMANZI in full, so your fee is already on our account before your day begins. There is no platform float and no waiting for the guest to pay through. Once admin clears, payout runs via tech-banking. Minutes to your account, not legacy banking queues.

M-Pesa is the standard channel. If you'd rather be paid by SEPA, bank transfer, or another method, just say so on the invoice — we accommodate.

For Concierge, your commission runs monthly for as long as the guest's subscription stays active. Amount confirmed during onboarding.

Do I need to front any costs?

No. Airport transfer is arranged and prepaid by KIMANZI. Fuel and lunch you advance on the day and log in your host portal — reimbursed in full.

Can I decline an assignment?

Yes, every time, without explanation. Declining one assignment has no effect on whether we offer you the next.

Do I have to be available on a schedule?

No. The "typical week pattern" question on the form helps us know roughly when not to call. You're free to be unavailable for weeks at a time, or to take a long break and pick up later.

What if a guest behaves out of scope during an assignment?

You stop, you call me — my number is in your assignment briefing. Go home. You are paid in full for the time you committed. KIMANZI handles the conversation with the guest from there. This isn't a guideline — it's the rule.

What if a guest asks me to do something outside the agreed assignment?

Do it only if you're comfortable, and let KIMANZI know — before if possible, right after if not. This isn't about permission. It's about keeping the picture complete so we can invoice correctly, follow up if needed, and make sure you're covered. Undisclosed work that later surfaces is harder to handle for everyone. A quick message is enough.

Do I need a social media presence?

No. We don't require it and we don't promote individual hosts publicly. The guest gets your name and a short professional background in their prep email — that's where it stays.

What does "independent in Kenya" mean for the form?

You work for yourself in Kenya. You have a KRA PIN, you invoice your clients, and you handle your own taxes. If you're still getting that in order, that's fine. Mark it honestly on the form. We've helped hosts navigate the process before and can point you in the right direction if needed.

How do I reach KIMANZI during an assignment?

Your assignment briefing includes a direct phone number for me. Day or evening, weekday or weekend, for anything urgent on the day. Call, don't wait. The number isn't published on this site to keep the line clear for hosts who are actually working on an assignment.

For applications and general questions outside an accepted assignment, the contact form on this page reaches me faster.

How often will I get assignments?

Honest answer: we don't promise a volume. Some months are quiet, some are busy. KIMANZI is meant to be an additional channel, not a primary income source. Hosts who like the model treat it as one of several streams.
